Hector Jimenez

For Val Verde USD Board of Education TA 2

About Me

Education

I was raised in Moreno Valley and Perris, and have lived in this community for over 23 years. I myself am a graduate of the the Val Verde Unified School District.

I began school at VVUSD as an english-language learner, and later graduated from Citrus Hill High School as a valedictorian. I went on to attend UC Riverside as a first-generation college student, where I graduated with a bachelor's degree in Computer Engineering.

I currenly work as a software engineer, and have worked as an engineer across several different industries since obtaining my degree.

Community Involvement

As a student in our school district, I experienced firsthand the positive impact that great teachers have on their students. These teachers inspired me to volunteer to tutor and teach students, and I actively volunteered beginning from my middle school years and all the way throughout college. I hope to continue to serve our community through policy on the school board and through being a role model for our students, showing them that it is possible to succeed regardless of their background.

Mission

Your Neighbor, Your Voice

This school district is our school district, and it works better when our community is involved. I will work to encourage and make it easier for the community to voice their input on school board decisions. I plan to advocate for students, and prioritize their well-being and success above any politics.

Our Children, Our Future

Our community’s children are our future leaders, doctors, engineers, and more. As such, our priority as a school district should be our children. My goal is to ensure that children in our community are given access to resources that will allow them to succeed in whatever career or education paths they chose. I will work on restoring and expanding rigorous coursework like AP classes, and partnering with local community colleges to offer technical career training options.

Our Staff, Our Community

Teachers have the power to change a student’s life, but teachers are not the only people needed to create a successful school district. Counselors, Nurses, Custodians, Police, Security, Cafeteria staff and more are all key to making our schools safe, clean, supportive, and effective learning spaces. The members of our community deserve to be treated well and compensated properly. I will negotiate with our employee’s unions in good faith, and make it a focus to shift any third-party contracted jobs to internal positions wherever possible.

A Safe and Inclusive School District

In a time of heavy political division, I want to ensure that our students can make their education their priority. No student deserves to feel like an outcast due to who they are or where they are from, or worry about whether they will be safe at school. I will work to ensure that we hold accountable those who fail to protect our students or create spaces where they may not feel safe. I plan to help ensure that schools have staff with the training needed to ensure student safety, while being mindful that their disciplinary actions do not do more harm than good to our students.
